The scaly boss works well, and the pointless bureaucracy is highlighted in its alienness.

I also really like the little alien details that come out all over the place, the characterisations of all the different race – over the top and cute.

I found the first reading slightly difficult – a little too dense, and the information was packaged in such a way that I sometimes could not tell if I had missed something or if the information wasn’t given to me on purpose. On second reading, much fewer qualms.

I have some minor suggestions :

– “The head of the Thrust Team limped to the front, his carapace still blackened from the testing incident, and said,” – you could try the paragraph without the “and said” – the meaning will remain clear.

– “Unfortunately all engineering and design specialists have been removed from the Steering Committee in order to streamline the product-to-market process. So I never told them (…)” – I’m not sure how the “so I never told them” follows from the preceding phrase.

– “Due to the cost in equipment and danger to key personnel all future thrust testing ” – I would suggest a comma after “future”

“Timidly the new intern raised a vivid orange pseudo-tentacle” – If you want that adverb up front, I would say it needs a comma after it. But something like “The new intern raised a timid orange pseudo-tentacle” may work better?

– The capitalisation of Steering Committee is inconsistent.

That seems like an awful number of nitpicks, but that is all they are – very small nitpicks to a very fun alien encounter.

If I have one regret it’s that I don’t know what the product is – I would have liked to have found it’s the starting kernel for our universe or something familiar that would tie up with the f*ck up of its creation… :)
