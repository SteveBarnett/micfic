]]> By: Rudy Neeser http://localhost:8888/microfiction.co.za/2010/07/07/the-presidential-soup/#comment-235 Wed, 14 Jul 2010 09:52:35 +0000 http://micfic.wordpress.com/?p=123#comment-235 This is about a country’s president sitting down to have a meal of a communally produced soup. The soup represents the nation’s feelings towards the president, and we learn the mechanics of this from the president’s memories. If the nation doesn’t like president, they’re perfectly able to poison the soup. The president then settles down in an attempt to enjoy the bisque that he has been served.

I enjoy both the idea of people having a direct say in how they feel their head of state is performing, and also the ceremony of it all. It’s fun to hear it playing out with the death of a former president.

One minor nit no one else has mentioned: I’m unsure about some of the semi-colons, and think that they could be better as colons. For example:

> I know I won’t enjoy the soup no matter the outcome[:] I never liked
> lobster bisque

and

> This bisque was made by a million hands[:] a gift of sorts
